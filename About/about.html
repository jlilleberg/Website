    My Story

    The ability to express myself has been at the core of every major decision I have made in my life. It is the reason I majored in Computer Science and Mathematics in which I strived to, and succeeded, in graduating Summa Cum Laude. I involved myself in these fields beyond coursework through leadership roles as President in their respective clubs, participated in NSF research at Georgia State University and University of North Texas, and attended competitive programming competitions at University of Nebraska - Lincoln. These feats led to my acceptance to prestigious graduate programs at both Columbia University and the University of California, San Diego where I opted for the latter for my Masters.

    After graduation, I took personal time off before applying for jobs in the fall of 2018. After months of interviews being unable to break into industry, I decided to take a step back and re-evaluate my approach. Considering there are not many opportunities in life where I would have this much free time, I wanted to explore the facets of Computer Science that I was unable to during my academic career. I spent the early half of 2019 studying to become a Full-Stack Web Developer, but the overall experience left a lot to be desired and was not career choice I could found myself to be truly passionate about.

    Being passionate in a career is very important to me because not only does it mean I'll be happier and more productive, I will go above and beyond to become a leader aiming to reach the quality and standards of those above me while bring those below up with me. Fortunately, this uncertainty didn't last long as I soon rekindled my passion for Artificial Intelligence. The reason I am so passionate about Artificial Intelligence is not only because it satifies my desires to solve difficult problems or that it allows me to grow as a mathematiican and computer scientist, but because it offers a precious and unique experience few, if any, other professions can, the ability for a few individuals to make a positive impact that affects so many.

    My first formal introduction to Artificial Intelligence was Principles of Artificial Intelligence: Probabilistic Reasoning and Learning at the University of California, San Diego. I found it both intriguing and exciting, eagerly wanting to learn more. Unfortunately, I was unable to take some of the courses I wanted to during my graduate experience. Fast forward to the summer of 2019, I started to take artificial intelligence courses. Over the following year, I have taken many Artificial Intelligence courses offered by Andrew Ng and Deeplearning.ai consisting of 4 specializations covering 20+ courses. In addition to the vast amount of projects I have worked on through the courses, I have also worked on many independent projects applying the concepts I have learned. I have completed Workera's standardized testing for AI Skills which scores you against practitioners in industry, including Amazon and Google, and top academic universities, such as Stanford and CMU, placing above the 80th percentile on average.

    Currently, I am actively applying for any data science related position with a particular interest in a Software Engineer - Machine Learning role. When I am not applying, I am working to expand my portfolio and machine learning skill set through projects. I am also available for contract work or to work on any machine learning or deep learning related open source work. If interested in an interview, working with me, or want to learn more about my story or any other inquisitions, I urge you to reach out and contact me.
