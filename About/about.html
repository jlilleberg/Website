    My Story

    The ability to express myself has been at the core of every major decision I have made in life. It is the reason I majored in Computer Science and Mathematics in which I strived to, and succeeded, in graduating Summa Cum Laude. I involved myself in these fields beyond coursework through leadership roles as President in their respective clubs, participated in NSF research at Georgia State University and University of North Texas, and attended competitive programming competitions at University of Nebraska - Lincoln. These feats led to my acceptance to prestigious graduate programs at both Columbia University and the University of California, San Deigo where I opted for the latter for my Masters.

    After graduation, I took personal time off before applying for jobs in the fall of 2018. After months of interviews being unable to break into industry, I decided to take a step back and re-evaluate my approach. Considering there are not many opportunities in life where I would have this much free time, I wanted to explore the the facets of Computer Science that I was unable to during my academic career. I spent the early half of 2019 studying to become a full-stack web developer, but as time passed I was convinced it was not a field I could be passionate about for the rest of my life. This uncertainty didn't last long as I soon discovered my passion for Artificial Intelligence which satisfied my desire to solve difficult problems by allowing me to expand my mathematical and computer science skillset and build innovative and expressive applications that can change the world.

    My first formal introduction to Artificial Intelligence was Principles of Artificial Intelligence: Probabilistic Reasoning and Learning at the University of California, San Diego. I found it both intriguing and exciting, eagerly wanting to learn more. Unfortunately, I was unable to take some of the courses I wanted to during my graduate expereince. Fastfoward to the summer of 2019, I started to take artificial intelligence courses. Over the following year, I have taken a myriad of artifical intelligence courses offered by Andrew Ng and Deeplearning.ai consisting of 4 specializations covering 20+ courses. In addition to the vast amount of projects I have worked on through the courses, I have also worked on many indepent projects applying the concepts I have learned. I have completed Workera's standardizd testing for AI Skills which scores you against practioners in industry, including Amazon and Google, and top academic universities, such as Stanford and CMU, placing above the 80th percentile on average.

    Currently, I am actively applying for any data science related position with a particular interest in a Software Engineer - Machine Learning position. When I am not applying, I am working to expand my portfolio and machine learning skillset through projects. I am also available for contract work or to work on any machine learning or deep learning related open source work. If you are interested in an interview, working with me, or want to learn more about my story or any other inquisitions, I encourage you to reach out and contact me, I would love to get to know you and what we can accomplish together!
